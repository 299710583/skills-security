## Contract Review Workflow

### Standard Contract Analysis Process

**Step 1: Document Intake**
- Read contract (PDF, DOCX, or plain text)
- Extract metadata: parties, date, contract type, jurisdiction

**Step 2: Clause Extraction**
- Identify key clauses:
  - **Parties**: Legal names, addresses, registration numbers
  - **Object**: Purpose and scope of contract
  - **Payment Terms**: Amount, currency, payment schedule
  - **Duration**: Start date, end date, renewal terms
  - **Termination**: Conditions for early termination
  - **Liability**: Limitation of liability clauses
  - **Dispute Resolution**: Arbitration/jurisdiction clauses
  - **Governing Law**: Applicable legal framework

**Step 3: Compliance Verification**
- Check against OHADA Uniform Acts
- Verify mandatory clauses are present
- Flag prohibited or risky terms
- Assess enforceability

**Step 4: Risk Assessment**
- **Legal Risk Score**: 1-10 (1=low, 10=critical)
- Risk categories:
  - Regulatory non-compliance
  - Unfavorable payment terms
  - Weak liability protection
  - Missing arbitration clause
  - Unclear termination rights

**Step 5: Recommendations**
- Suggest clause modifications
- Propose additional protections
- Reference OHADA/CEMAC standards
- Provide template alternatives

## Regulatory Compliance Checks

### AML/KYC Compliance Verification

**Use Case**: Verify customer onboarding meets CEMAC AML requirements

**Required Documents**:
- Government-issued ID (passport, national ID card)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement < 3 months)
- Source of funds declaration (for high-value accounts)
- Business registration (for corporate accounts)

**Verification Steps**:
1. **Identity Verification**
   - Validate ID document authenticity
   - Check expiry date
   - Verify name, date of birth, nationality

2. **PEP Screening**
   - Check against politically exposed persons database
   - Enhanced due diligence if PEP identified
   - Source of wealth verification required

3. **Sanctions Screening**
   - UN sanctions lists
   - OFAC (US Treasury)
   - EU sanctions lists
   - National sanctions lists

4. **Risk Classification**
   ```
   Low Risk: Salaried employees, domestic transactions
   Medium Risk: Self-employed, cross-border transactions
   High Risk: PEPs, high-value transactions, cash-intensive businesses
   ```

5. **Ongoing Monitoring**
   - Transaction monitoring for suspicious patterns
   - Periodic KYC updates (annually for high-risk, every 3 years for low-risk)
   - Suspicious activity reporting (SAR) if needed

## Error Handling

### Common Issues & Solutions

**Issue**: Contract language not supported
```
Error: Contract in English, but OHADA contracts must be in French
Solution: Translate contract or request French version
Note: OHADA official language is French for legal validity
```

**Issue**: Invalid company registration number (RCCM)
```
Error: RCCM number format invalid (expected: CM-YDE-2025-A-0001)
Solution: Verify format with national RCCM registry
Format: [COUNTRY]-[CITY]-[YEAR]-[TYPE]-[NUMBER]
```

**Issue**: Arbitration clause refers to non-OHADA jurisdiction
```
Error: Arbitration in Paris ICC court (not CCJA)
Warning: May not be enforceable in OHADA countries
Recommendation: Use CCJA arbitration for OHADA compliance
```

**Issue**: Missing mandatory clauses
```
Error: No governing law clause specified
Requirement: OHADA contracts must specify applicable law
Solution: Add "Governed by OHADA Uniform Acts" clause
```
